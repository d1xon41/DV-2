00 — GLOBAL CONTEXT

Australia in the world

Australia is one of the world's 17 megadiverse countries, hosting over 10% of all species on Earth. Its national parks sit at the heart of global biodiversity conservation.

Australia's Protected Areas in Global Context

Australia (shown in green) contains some of the world's most ecologically significant protected areas, spanning tropical, desert, and temperate ecosystems.

02 — QUANTITY

Where are the most protected areas?

03 — CLASSIFICATION

How are protected areas classified?

The IUCN system categorises protected areas from strict nature reserves (Ia) to sustainable use areas (VI). Category VI dominates Australia's reserve system by area.

Area by IUCN Protection Category

Category VI accounts for the vast majority of Australia's protected area, reflecting large Indigenous Protected Areas.

IUCN Category Breakdown by State

Each state has a distinct protection profile — Victoria favours strict reserves while WA and SA rely on Category VI areas.

04 — GOVERNANCE

Who manages Australia's protected areas?

Protected areas are managed by a mix of government agencies, Indigenous communities, and private landholders under different governance frameworks.

Protected Areas by Governance Type

Government-managed areas dominate in number, but Indigenous and private governance cover significant land area.

How Australia Uses Its Land

Nature conservation accounts for 22.6% of Australia's total land area, competing with grazing, forestry, and agriculture.

05 — BIOREGIONS

Which bioregions are best protected?

Australia's 89 IBRA bioregions vary enormously in protection levels. Desert bioregions in WA and NT dominate by total protected area.

Top 15 Bioregions by Protected Area

The Great Victoria Desert and Great Sandy Desert have the largest absolute protected areas in Australia.

Visitor Trends at Major Parks

COVID-19 caused a sharp drop in visitors across all major parks in 2020, with recovery varying significantly by location.

06 — EXTINCTION CRISIS

Australia's record of species loss

Australia has one of the world's worst extinction records. Mammals have been hit hardest, with dozens of species lost since European settlement.

Species Extinctions by Decade

Extinction rates peaked in the early 1900s as land clearing and introduced predators spread across the continent.

Total Extinctions by Group

Mammals account for the majority of Australian extinctions — a pattern unlike almost any other country on Earth.

Australia's protected areas at a glance

The national reserve system spans every state and territory, from Antarctic territories to tropical rainforests.

National Reserve System

Australia's network of protected areas covers over 173 million hectares across all jurisdictions.
